Business Overview
  • Alphabet (NASDAQ: GOOGL) is the holding company for Google plus 'Other Bets' moonshots, valued at ~$2.1T (2026-06-03 close).
  • Q1 2026 revenue was $109.9B (+22% YoY), an 11th straight quarter of double-digit growth.
  • Google Services: $89.6B (+16%), Search & other $60.4B (+19%), YouTube ads $9.88B, subscriptions/platforms/devices $12.4B.
  • Google Cloud: $20.0B (+63%), with enterprise AI solutions the primary Cloud growth driver for the first time.
  • Operating margin expanded to 36.1%; net income $62.6B (+81%); Cloud backlog/RPO topped $460B.
  • Full-stack AI thesis: Gemini frontier models + custom TPU silicon + global data centers + distribution across Search/Android/Workspace/Cloud.
  • TPU is becoming a sellable Nvidia alternative: Anthropic up to ~$40B/5GW, Meta in talks, a Blackstone neocloud JV.
  • 2026 capex guided to $180-190B; twin antitrust overhangs (DOJ Search appeal + adtech remedy) persist.

Product & Revenue Model
  • Advertising is the cash core (Search $60.4B +19%, YouTube ads $9.88B, Network), sold on auction/CPC/CPM economics.
  • Google Cloud ($20.0B, +63%) monetizes consumption GCP infrastructure/platform plus Workspace SaaS, increasingly driven by enterprise AI infra (TPU/GPU compute) and AI solutions (Gemini Enterprise, Vertex AI). Subscriptions/platforms/devices ($12.4B) covers YouTube Premium/TV, Google One, Play take-rate, and Pixel.
  • Gemini is monetized directly via API (>16B tokens/minute, +60% QoQ) and embedded across consumer and enterprise surfaces; serving unit costs fell ~78% over 2025.

Key Risks & Debates
  • AI-native query disruption: generative answers could erode long-term Search ad clicks even as near-term query volume rises.
  • Capital intensity: $180-190B 2026 capex more than doubles 2025 and pressures near-term FCF if AI revenue lags.
  • Antitrust: DOJ/35-state Search appeal seeks tougher remedies; the separate adtech case still threatens an ad-exchange divestiture.
  • TPU supply is a two-edged sword: selling record external capacity (Anthropic, Meta) leaves DeepMind queuing for its own compute.
  • Cloud is #3 and must keep closing the gap on AWS/Azure to justify the re-rating.
  • Other Bets remain a ~$2.1B/quarter operating loss with Waymo still pre-scale.
  • Valuation: after crossing $4T, the stock prices in continued AI and Cloud execution.
