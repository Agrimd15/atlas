Business Overview
  • Microsoft (NASDAQ: MSFT) is the world's largest software company and a top-two hyperscale cloud/AI platform, ~$3.2T market cap (2026-06-03 close).
  • FQ3 2026 (ended Mar 31, 2026) revenue was $82.9B (+18% YoY); diluted EPS $4.27.
  • Microsoft Cloud crossed $50B in a quarter for the first time, at $54.5B (+29%).
  • Azure reaccelerated to +40% YoY (+39% cc), beating the company's own 37-38% guidance.
  • AI revenue run-rate surpassed $37B annualized, up ~123% YoY; M365 Copilot paid seats topped 20M.
  • The bear case is capital intensity: ~$190B calendar-2026 capex (~$35B above consensus, ~$25B from memory inflation) sent shares down ~3%.
  • April 2026 OpenAI restructuring removed Azure exclusivity and IP revenue-share, extended IP licensing to 2032, framed as margin-accretive.
  • Seven in-house MAI models launched at Build 2026 signal a push toward long-term AI self-sufficiency.

Product & Revenue Model
  • Recurring subscriptions (M365 commercial/consumer, Dynamics, LinkedIn, plus M365 Copilot and GitHub Copilot per-seat AI) form the high-margin base.
  • Consumption cloud, Azure compute, storage, and AI inference/training, is the primary growth driver, billed on usage.
  • Transactional/device/advertising covers Windows OEM, Surface, search/news ads, and Xbox.
  • AI monetizes both as premium per-seat SKUs (~$30/user/month) and metered Azure AI/OpenAI consumption, with management moving toward more consumption-based pricing over time.

Key Risks & Debates
  • Capital intensity: ~$190B calendar-2026 capex (~$35B above consensus) risks depreciation outrunning AI revenue and compressing operating leverage.
  • Memory/component inflation: ~$25B of the capex increase is price, not capacity, a sign of cost pressure across the AI supply chain.
  • Monetization proof: M365 Copilot is only ~3.3% penetrated; the full per-seat-to-consumption monetization curve is unproven.
  • OpenAI relationship: the restructured deal removes exclusivity and adds OpenAI as a partial competitor as Microsoft builds its own MAI models.
  • Pricing-model shift: moving Copilot toward consumption pricing adds revenue-timing variability.
  • Legacy drag: More Personal Computing is flat-to-down on a soft PC backdrop.
  • Valuation: at 10.1x EV/Rev the recovery path is tied to sustained Azure/Copilot acceleration.
