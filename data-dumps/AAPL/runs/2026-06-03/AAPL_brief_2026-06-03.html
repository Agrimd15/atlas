Business Overview
  • Apple (NASDAQ: AAPL) is the world's largest consumer-devices company by revenue, at ~$4.6T market cap (2026-06-03 close), among the most valuable of the big-tech mega-caps.
  • FQ2 2026 (ended Mar 28, 2026) set a record March quarter: revenue $111.2B (+17% YoY), EPS $2.01 (+22%), gross margin 49.3% (up from 47.1%).
  • All five product categories and all five geographies grew, led by 'off the charts' iPhone 17 demand.
  • Services hit an all-time record $31.0B (+16.3%), ~28% of revenue and the key gross-margin driver.
  • Greater China rebounded sharply to $20.5B (+28%) on iPhone 17 strength and local subsidies.
  • Historic leadership transition: Tim Cook becomes Executive Chair and John Ternus becomes CEO effective Sept 1, 2026.
  • AI is the catch-up story: a ~$1B/year Google Gemini deal will power a revamped Siri (Phase 1 iOS 26.5, fuller version iOS 27).
  • Forward margin watch: management flagged 'significantly higher' memory costs as suppliers redirect capacity to AI chips.

Product & Revenue Model
  • iPhone (~51% of revenue, $57.0B in FQ2'26, +21.7%) anchors premium hardware alongside Mac, iPad and Wearables/Home/Accessories.
  • The Services layer ($31.0B, +16.3%, all-time record) spans App Store, AppleCare, advertising, cloud, payments and subscriptions, carrying far higher gross margins and driving total company gross margin to 49.3%. A record active-device installed base provides a recurring-revenue flywheel.
  • Hardware is contract-manufactured (Foxconn, Pegatron), with assembly shifting toward India and Vietnam; processors are fabricated exclusively by TSMC.

June Quarter Guidance
Revenue up 14-17% YoY
  • AI / Product: Apple positioned 2026 as its AI catch-up year. Management pointed to the Gemini-powered Siri rolling out in iOS 26.5 with a fuller conversational version targeted for iOS 27, leaning on the multiyear ~$1B/year Google partnership while emphasizing on-device processing and Private Cloud Compute for privacy.
  • Demand: Demand was the standout: Cook called iPhone 17 demand 'off the charts' and the 'most popular lineup in our history,' with iPhone setting a March-quarter record despite supply constraints on advanced TSMC nodes and the memory shortage. All five categories and all five geographies grew, with Greater China up 28%. June-quarter revenue is guided up 14-17%, but memory costs will be 'significantly higher' next quarter, a forward gross-margin headwind.
Key Risks & Debates
  • AI lag: outsourcing core Siri to Google Gemini (~$1B/year) and repeated delays raise questions about Apple's long-term model independence and differentiation.
  • Margin headwind: management flagged 'significantly higher' memory costs as makers redirect capacity to AI chips.
  • Supply constraints: iPhone 17 demand outran supply on tight TSMC advanced nodes and memory, leaving revenue on the table.
  • Geopolitics: 50% US tariffs on India and a threatened 25% tariff on non-US iPhones pressure the China-diversification strategy.
  • Regulation: EU DMA App Store fee restructurings (5% Core Technology Commission) plus a EUR500M fine threaten high-margin Services economics.
  • Leadership transition risk: a new CEO (Ternus) takes over Sept 1, 2026.
  • Concentration: iPhone is still ~51% of revenue; the AI super-cycle for hardware is unproven.
