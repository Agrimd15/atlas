Business Overview
Plain English
  • Decagon makes AI 'agents' that talk to a company's customers and actually solve their problems.
  • Instead of a dumb chatbot that only answers FAQs, Decagon's AI can do real tasks like issue a refund, cancel a subscription, or reschedule a delivery.
  • It works over chat, email, phone, and text, and companies pay mostly only when the AI fully fixes the customer's issue on its own.
The technical version
  • Decagon is an enterprise agent platform that orchestrates frontier LLMs (OpenAI, Anthropic, Cohere) plus proprietary fine-tuned models behind natural-language Agent Operating Procedures (AOPs).
  • Agents are wired into a customer's ticketing systems, knowledge bases, and operational APIs so they can both reason over context and execute state-changing actions, achieving ~70-75% fully autonomous resolution across voice, chat, email, and SMS. Decagon deploys via a forward-deployed engineering model and monetizes on a per-conversation or higher-value per-resolution (outcome) basis rather than per-seat.
Explained simply
  • Think of Decagon as hiring a tireless, all-knowing front-desk concierge for a company's customer service, except it's software.
  • Like a great concierge, it doesn't just point you to a help page; it picks up the phone or chat, figures out what you need, and handles it (the refund, the cancellation, the rebooking) on the spot, 24/7.
  • And the company is billed a bit like tipping the concierge only when the job actually gets done.
  • Decagon is a San Francisco-based, AI-native customer-service company founded in August 2023 by Jesse Zhang (CEO) and Ashwin Sreenivas (President) that builds enterprise 'AI concierge' agents.
  • Its agents autonomously resolve roughly 70-75% of customer-support conversations end-to-end across voice, chat, email and SMS, taking real actions in backend systems such as refunds, cancellations and disputes rather than just answering questions.
  • The product is configured through natural-language Agent Operating Procedures so non-technical CX teams can build and tune agents, and it is deployed via a forward-deployed engineering model with custom integrations per customer.
  • Decagon monetizes on consumption, primarily a per-conversation or higher-value per-resolution model of roughly $1.50 per resolution, about 10% of a human agent's cost.
  • ARR scaled more than 3x year over year to about $35M by October 2025 from roughly $10M at end-2024, across 100+ enterprise logos including Notion, Duolingo, Affirm, Chime, Rippling, Hertz, Avis and Deutsche Telekom.
  • The company has raised about $481M from a16z, Accel, Bain Capital Ventures, Coatue and Index Ventures, with its valuation tripling from $1.5B to $4.5B in roughly seven months via a $250M Series D in January 2026.
  • Decagon sits at the front of the agentic customer-experience category alongside Sierra, which raised $950M at $15.8B in May 2026 on similar outcome-based pricing.
  • The central debate is durability of the moat: because Decagon orchestrates third-party frontier models, defensibility rests on workflow depth, enterprise integrations and a concierge data flywheel rather than the LLMs themselves.

Product & Revenue Model
  • Decagon sells an enterprise AI-agent platform for building, optimizing and scaling customer-facing agents, sold predominantly to large support and CX organizations via a forward-deployed engineering model.
  • Agents are defined in natural-language Agent Operating Procedures and wired into ticketing systems, knowledge bases and operational APIs so they can both reason over context and execute state-changing actions.
  • The platform is omnichannel across voice (launched via an ElevenLabs partnership), chat, email and SMS, with cross-channel memory.
  • The underlying stack is model-agnostic, routing across OpenAI, Anthropic and Google frontier models plus proprietary fine-tunes, with a supervisor model running guardrail checks before responses reach customers.
  • Tooling spans Watchtower monitoring, Insights and Reporting, Experiments and Testing/QA, plus Proactive Agents for outbound and lifecycle engagement.
  • Revenue is consumption-based: a per-conversation model with volume discounts, or a higher-value per-resolution model that charges only on successful autonomous resolution with no charge on escalation.

Decagon now spans voice, chat, email and SMS, noting that voice raises the bar because 'you need the latency to be super crisp.'.

The pricing question is live too: Zhang concedes most customers prefer per-conversation over per-resolution because resolution-based billing creates messy edge cases, which tempers the cleanest 'pay for outcomes' narrative.
Key Risks & Debates
  • Valuation: the $4.5B Series D mark is roughly 129x the ~$35M ARR run-rate, pricing in years of flawless execution and exposed to any compression in AI-application multiples.
  • Model commoditization: Decagon orchestrates third-party frontier models (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google), so the moat must come from workflow depth, integrations and the AOP data flywheel, not the LLMs themselves.
  • Incumbent bundling: Salesforce (Agentforce), Intercom (Fin) and Zendesk can attach good-enough agents to the systems of record and helpdesks customers already pay for.
  • Sierra arms race: the closest peer raised $950M at $15.8B in May 2026 on near-identical outcome pricing, escalating the capital, talent and logo contest.
  • Pricing tension: management concedes most customers prefer per-conversation over per-resolution billing, which muddies the clean pay-for-outcomes narrative and complicates ARR quality.
  • Voice dependency: the phone channel relies on an ElevenLabs partnership, a buy-versus-build choice that raises gross-margin and third-party-dependency questions.
  • Forward-deployed scaling: heavy custom implementation per customer drives expansion but pressures gross margin and onboarding speed as the logo count grows.
