Business Overview
In one sentenceDatabricks is the private data-and-AI company behind the 'lakehouse', one platform unifying a company's data engineering, warehousing, analytics, and AI so teams build everything from dashboards to AI agents on a single copy of their data. >$5.4B revenue run-rate (>65% YoY); $134B last round.
Plain English
What it does, who pays
  • Databricks gives big companies one place to store all their data and build AI on it.
  • It blends a cheap 'data lake' with a fast 'warehouse', the combo it calls a lakehouse.
  • Teams use it for dashboards, machine learning, and now AI chatbots and agents.
  • It's private, growing >65% a year at a >$5.4B run-rate, valued at $134B+.
The technical version
How it actually works
  • Lakehouse = open data-lake storage (Delta Lake/Iceberg) with warehouse-grade SQL and governance (Unity Catalog).
  • Built on Apache Spark for distributed compute and MLflow for ML; runs on AWS, Azure, GCP.
  • Mosaic AI handles fine-tuning, RAG, vector search, serving and agents (Agent Bricks); Claude is native.
  • Consumption (DBU) pricing with >140% net retention; Lakebase adds serverless Postgres for agent workloads.
Explained simply
The analogy
  • Think of a giant, organized digital warehouse where a company keeps every piece of its data.
  • Databricks is the warehouse plus the smart robots that find answers and build AI on those shelves.
  • Instead of shipping your data out to an AI, you bring the AI into your own warehouse.
Key terms, decoded
Lakehouse
Databricks' architecture that combines a cheap data lake's open storage with a data warehouse's fast, governed querying, so you don't need two separate systems.
Data warehouse
A structured, optimized database for fast business analytics and reporting on cleaned, organized data.
Data lake
A low-cost store that holds raw data of any type (structured or not) at massive scale before it's organized.
Apache Spark
The open-source engine (created by Databricks' founders) that processes huge datasets in parallel across many machines.
Delta Lake
Databricks' open storage format that adds reliability (transactions, versioning) on top of a data lake.
Unity Catalog
Databricks' governance layer that controls who can access which data and AI assets across the platform.
ETL Extract, Transform, Load
Pulling data from sources, cleaning/reshaping it, and loading it where it can be analyzed.
Run-rate (ARR) Annualized Run-Rate / Annual Recurring Revenue
A yearly-revenue estimate from annualizing current recurring or consumption revenue, useful for a fast-growing private company between reports.
GenAI / LLM Generative AI / Large Language Model
AI models (like Claude) that generate text, code or answers, the foundation of the chatbots and agents Databricks helps companies build.
NRR Net Revenue Retention
How much more (or less) existing customers spend year over year; >100% means expansion beats churn, Databricks is >140%.
  • Databricks, founded in 2013 by the UC Berkeley creators of Apache Spark (Ali Ghodsi, Matei Zaharia, Ion Stoica, Reynold Xin and others), is the company behind the 'Data Intelligence Platform', its branding of the lakehouse architecture that merges the cheap, open storage of a data lake with the structured query performance and governance of a data warehouse.
  • Built on the open-source projects it created or stewards (Apache Spark, Delta Lake, MLflow, Unity Catalog) plus the open table format world it expanded into via Iceberg (the Tabular acquisition), the platform lets enterprises run data engineering, BI/SQL warehousing, streaming, and AI/ML on one governed copy of their data across AWS, Azure and Google Cloud.
  • Since 2023 Databricks has pivoted hard into generative AI through its Mosaic AI stack (from the ~$1.3B MosaicML acquisition), letting customers build, fine-tune and serve LLM-powered applications and agents directly on their data, and added a serverless operational database (Lakebase) via the ~$1B Neon acquisition.
  • It is the lead challenger to Snowflake in the data-platform wars and is headquartered in San Francisco.
  • As of 2026 it remains private, with a revenue run-rate above $5.4B (>65% YoY) and a $134B post-money valuation, while reportedly in talks for a new round at $165-175B.

Product & Revenue Model
  • Databricks prices on consumption (usage), not seats: customers pay for the compute they consume, metered in Databricks Units (DBUs), on top of the underlying cloud, across AWS, Azure (first-party 'Azure Databricks') and Google Cloud.
  • The platform is layered: (1) the lakehouse foundation, Delta Lake open storage plus Unity Catalog governance over a customer's data lake; (2) Databricks SQL / Data Warehousing, serverless SQL analytics that crossed a >$1B run-rate in under four years; (3) Mosaic AI, model building, fine-tuning, RAG, vector search, model serving and Agent Bricks for production AI agents, including native access to Anthropic's Claude models via a five-year partnership; and (4) Lakebase, serverless Postgres (from Neon) as the operational/transactional database for AI agents, growing roughly 2x the pace of the warehousing business.
  • Genie brings natural-language querying to enterprise data.
  • Net revenue retention exceeds 140% via a land-and-expand motion; AI products alone now run above a $1.4B annualized run-rate.

Key Risks & Debates
  • Valuation vs. revenue (rich multiple): at a $134B valuation (and reportedly $165-175B in talks) on a >$5.4B run-rate, Databricks trades at roughly ~25-32x run-rate revenue, far richer than public data peers, leaving little room for a growth stumble before an IPO.
  • Hyperscaler competition: AWS (Redshift/SageMaker), Microsoft Fabric, and Google BigQuery bundle data + AI natively into the clouds Databricks runs on, and can subsidize or pre-install rival stacks, a structural channel and pricing threat.
  • Snowflake rivalry: the direct lakehouse-vs-warehouse competitor is converging on Databricks' turf (open Iceberg tables, Cortex AI, its own LLM features), pressuring differentiation and pricing in the core analytics market.
  • IPO timing & late-stage investor pressure: successive mega-rounds at ever-higher marks raise the bar the IPO must clear; Ghodsi points to ~2027, prolonging the liquidity overhang for employees and crossover backers.
  • AI-platform execution & monetization durability: the AI run-rate (>$1.4B) is growing fast but young, and consumption can be cut quickly in a downturn, proving Mosaic AI/Agent Bricks/Lakebase become durable, high-margin lines (not experimental spend) is unproven at scale.
  • Open-source commoditization: Databricks' moat is built on open projects it no longer solely controls (Spark, Delta Lake, and now Iceberg via Tabular), open table formats and commoditized compute could let customers replicate core capabilities or migrate workloads more cheaply over time.

SWOT Analysis
How Databricks stands out: Databricks is the only at-scale, multi-cloud, open lakehouse that has turned its open-source roots (Spark, Delta, Iceberg) into a >$5.4B-run-rate, FCF-positive franchise growing >65%, uniquely positioned to ride enterprise AI by bringing models to a customer's governed data rather than the reverse. Public proxy Snowflake trades at ~16.5x EV/Rev (2026-06-10 close); Databricks' last private round implies a richer ~25-32x run-rate mark.
StrengthsInternal edge
  • Open lakehouse + multi-cloud reach: Runs natively on AWS, Azure (first-party) and GCP on open formats (Delta Lake, Iceberg), avoiding lock-in and spanning a customer's entire data + AI estate on one governed copy.
  • Hypergrowth at scale with FCF: >$5.4B run-rate growing >65% YoY while FCF-positive over the trailing 12 months, a rare combination of scale, acceleration and profitability.
  • AI distribution & data gravity: Mosaic AI + native Claude (Anthropic) + Agent Bricks + Lakebase let 20,000+ enterprises build AI on data they already store in Databricks; >140% net retention shows the engine compounding.
WeaknessesInternal gap
  • Rich private multiple: ~25-32x run-rate revenue at $134B (more at $165-175B) leaves little margin for error and a demanding bar for the eventual IPO.
  • Consumption-model cyclicality: Usage-based revenue can be throttled quickly by customers in a downturn, making the run-rate more volatile than seat-based SaaS.
  • Liquidity overhang: No public stock and a delayed IPO (2027+) means employees and crossover investors face prolonged illiquidity, managed via periodic tender offers.
OpportunitiesExternal upside
  • Operational-data TAM (Lakebase): The Neon-powered serverless Postgres extends Databricks from analytics into live application/agent data, a large new market growing ~2x the warehousing business.
  • Enterprise agentic AI: Agent Bricks + Genie position Databricks to be the platform of record for production AI agents over enterprise data as that workload explodes.
  • IPO optionality: A marquee crossover syndicate and 'IPO-ready' governance set up a high-profile listing (potentially 2027) that could re-rate the whole category.
ThreatsExternal risk
  • Hyperscaler bundling: AWS, Microsoft Fabric and Google BigQuery bundle competing data+AI stacks into the clouds Databricks depends on, and can compete on price and distribution.
  • Snowflake convergence: Snowflake is adopting open tables (Iceberg) and native AI (Cortex), eroding Databricks' openness and AI differentiation in the core market.
  • Open-source commoditization: The open formats that are Databricks' moat (Spark, Delta, Iceberg) also lower switching costs and let rivals replicate core capabilities.
