Business Overview
  • C3.ai is an enterprise AI software company providing the C3 Agentic AI Platform, industry-specific SaaS AI applications, and generative AI offerings to large enterprises and government agencies.
  • Founded 2009 by Tom Siebel; IPO December 2020 (NYSE: AI). FY2025 revenue of $389M (+25% YoY) was the high-water mark - FY2026 revenue collapsed to ~$250M (-36%) following a disruptive CEO transition from Siebel to Stephen Ehikian (Sep 2025) and back to Siebel (May 2026).
  • Federal segment is the standout growth engine: 51 federal agreements closed in FY2025, federal bookings up 100%+ YoY, representing ~20% of total bookings. Federal/defense/aerospace was 45% of Q2 FY2026 bookings.
  • Primary go-to-market: hyperscaler partnerships (Microsoft Azure, AWS, Google Cloud) plus SI alliances (PwC, McKinsey QuantumBlack) now account for 73% of FY2025 agreements.

Product & Revenue Model
  • Subscription licenses (~86% of revenue in FY2025) for the C3 Agentic AI Platform and industry-specific applications across manufacturing, energy, healthcare, financial services, and federal.
  • Professional services and prioritized engineering (IPDs) supplement subscriptions but compress gross margins (non-GAAP GM ~69% FY2025 but dropped to ~52% in Q1 FY2026 due to high IPD mix). C3.ai does not report ARR or NRR - moved to consumption/agreement-count metrics.

Key Risks & Debates
  • Leadership instability: Siebel → Ehikian → Siebel in under a year destroyed go-to-market continuity and resulted in a 36% revenue decline in FY2026 - recovery trajectory is highly uncertain
  • Revenue collapse not yet reversed: FY2026 full-year at $250M is below FY2023 levels ($267M) - the company is currently regressing on the top line
  • M&A uncertainty: Automation Anywhere merger talks and strategic review process create headline risk and management distraction at a critical rebuild moment
  • Gross margin compression: Q1 FY2026 non-GAAP GM of 52% vs. 69% in Q4 FY2025 - IPD-heavy go-to-market strategy structurally pressures margins
  • Palantir gap widening: PLTR is profitable, growing 63% YoY, trading at 55x revenue - C3.ai at 3-4x revenue with negative growth is difficult to position as a peer
  • China/federal concentration: Federal is now 45% of bookings - export control policy shifts and government budget cycles create binary revenue risk

Slide-Ready Bullets
  1. C3.ai's FY2026 revenue collapsed to ~$250M (-36% YoY) from $389M in FY2025 - the steepest single-year decline among public enterprise AI software companies - driven entirely by a disruptive founder-CEO transition and sales reorg
  2. Tom Siebel resumed the CEO role in May 2026 after Stephen Ehikian's 8-month tenure; the board is now executing a recovery with $675M cash, a leaner cost structure (-312 headcount), and a refocus on the federal segment (45% of Q2 FY2026 bookings)
  3. Federal/government is the only reliably growing segment - US government/defense/aerospace bookings grew 89% YoY in Q2 FY2026, positioning C3.ai as a de facto enterprise AI infrastructure layer for agency digitization
  4. Agentic AI product pivot (C3 Agentic Process Automation, C3 Code, Agentic Websites) launched in late 2025 targets a higher-multiple, stickier category - but commercial traction has not yet offset the sales disruption
  5. M&A optionality is the near-term investor thesis: Automation Anywhere merger talks (Jan 2026) and a strategic review process suggest the board is weighing all options; at ~$1.2B market cap and $675M cash, the floor valuation is well-supported
Smart Diligence Questions
  1. What does the commercial pipeline look like under Siebel's return - specifically, are the customer relationships disrupted during the Ehikian tenure (73% of FY2025 agreements came via channel partners) being actively recovered, and what is the current channel partner sentiment?
  2. How sustainable is the federal revenue growth at 45% of bookings? What is the customer concentration within the federal segment (e.g., reliance on a single agency or CDAO vehicle), and what is the ceiling for this segment given C3.ai's current GSA schedule and contract vehicles?
  3. What are the actual terms and current status of the Automation Anywhere merger discussions - is this a live deal, a dead conversation, or a negotiating signal - and how does it impact management bandwidth during the most critical rebuild quarter?
