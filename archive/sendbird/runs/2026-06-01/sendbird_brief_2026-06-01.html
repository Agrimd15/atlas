Business Overview
  • Sendbird is a cloud communications API and SDK platform enabling developers to embed in-app chat, voice, video, and AI-powered customer engagement into mobile and web applications.
  • Founded 2013 (YC alum); Series C unicorn at $1.05B (April 2021). ~$40M ARR in 2024 with 12% YoY growth - modest for a unicorn, indicating core chat API business has matured.
  • Processes 7B+ conversations per month across 4,000+ customer applications and 300M+ monthly active users. Key customers: DoorDash, Match Group, Noom, Yahoo Sports.
  • 2025 strategic pivot to AI: launched Delight.ai (AI concierge with long-term memory, Nov 2025), Voice AI agents in 29 languages (Aug 2025), and signed a 3-year AWS Strategic Collaboration Agreement (Aug 2025).

Product & Revenue Model
  • Usage-based SaaS API platform.
  • Customers pay based on monthly active users (MAUs) and message volume.
  • Revenue through tiered subscriptions plus consumption overages.
  • Three product layers: (1) Communications APIs - chat, voice, video SDKs; (2) Business Messaging - omnichannel SMS, WhatsApp, push, in-app notifications (launched April 2024, processed 5.6B messages in first 6 months); (3) AI Agents - Delight.ai concierge + Voice AI (2025). AWS Marketplace listing as launch partner for the new AI Agents & Tools category.

Key Risks & Debates
  • Growth deceleration: 12% ARR growth in 2024 is well below expectations for a $1B unicorn - company needs the AI agent pivot to meaningfully inflect revenue within 12-18 months or faces a down-round or distressed exit scenario
  • No new funding in 5 years: last raise was April 2021; SoftBank Vision Fund 2 and Tiger Global are the largest institutional holders, both under LP pressure to return capital - liquidity timeline is a real question
  • Commoditization risk: OpenAI's real-time API and LLM-native entrants could commoditize the voice/chat layer that Sendbird currently monetizes - Delight.ai must succeed as a differentiated CX platform, not just an API wrapper
  • Scale gap vs. Twilio: Twilio is at $5.1B revenue (+14% YoY) vs. Sendbird's $40M ARR - competing for enterprise budgets as an omnichannel messaging platform is structurally difficult against a 100x larger incumbent
  • Stream competitive pressure: Stream (direct in-app chat API competitor) is gaining on developer experience and price; developer-first markets have low switching costs
  • Valuation reset risk: $1.05B valuation at 10x ARR set in April 2021 at peak multiples; at current market (5-7x ARR for similar-growth private SaaS), fair value is closer to $200-280M - significant markdown risk in any new round

Smart Diligence Questions
  1. What is Sendbird's NRR from its top 20 enterprise logos (DoorDash, Match, Noom) - specifically, are these customers expanding into Business Messaging and Delight.ai, or is usage flat? NRR expansion from existing logos is the most credible near-term reacceleration lever.
  2. How is Delight.ai priced and what is the average ACV uplift vs. the base chat API subscription? If the AI concierge layer can 3-5x ACV per enterprise customer, the $40M ARR base can re-rate quickly - but if it's an add-on with modest uptake, the growth thesis doesn't hold.
  3. What are the founder and investor liquidity timelines - specifically, SoftBank Vision Fund 2 and Tiger Global's appetite for a new round at a reset valuation vs. a strategic sale? Five years post-Series C with modest growth creates real pressure to find a path to liquidity in 2025-2026.
