Business Overview
  • Anaplan is a cloud-native connected planning platform for enterprise finance, sales, supply chain, and HR, the largest independent pure-play EPM vendor by ARR.
  • Founded 2006, taken private by Thoma Bravo in June 2022 at $10.7B EV (~14x ARR). ARR crossed $1B as of early 2025, up from ~$700M at go-private.
  • Platform is powered by the proprietary Hyperblock multidimensional calculation engine, deeply embedded in enterprise workflows with high switching costs.
  • Three BUs: Finance (>$600M ARR, core; 9x Gartner Magic Quadrant Leader), Sales & Marketing (>$200M ARR), Supply Chain (~$200M ARR, fastest-growing).
  • 200+ customers with >$1M ACV, representing more than half of aggregate ARR. Customers include ~half of the Fortune 50.
  • Preparing confidential IPO filing as of January 2026 per The Information, likely 2026 public market re-entry if market conditions cooperate.

Key Risks & Debates
  • Growth deceleration under Thoma Bravo: ARR CAGR of ~13-15% since go-private vs. 24%+ public-era growth, re-IPO story depends on showing re-acceleration
  • Competitive displacement by Pigment (fast-growing AI-native challenger) eating into Anaplan's mid-market and emerging enterprise accounts
  • OneStream (now Hg-backed) dominant in financial close/consolidation after Fluence acquisition, direct overlap with Anaplan's expanded suite
  • PE ownership debt load: Thoma Bravo typically leverages buyouts; high-interest debt servicing could constrain R&D investment relative to better-capitalized public peers
  • NRR unknown: public-era NRR was ~120%+ but post-go-private efficiency initiatives (headcount reductions, S&M cuts) may have pressured net retention in the near-term
  • Workday Adaptive Planning bundled into HCM suite creates price-compression risk in competitive deals where Workday is already the HCM vendor

Smart Diligence Questions
  1. What is the current NRR and how has it trended since the go-private? The PE efficiency playbook (S&M cuts, headcount reductions) often pressures net retention in the near-term, this is the single most important metric for the re-IPO story.
  2. How is Anaplan structurally defending against Pigment at the enterprise level? Pigment is doubling ARR annually and winning 57% of its new revenue from enterprise, what is Anaplan's win rate in competitive bake-offs post-2023?
