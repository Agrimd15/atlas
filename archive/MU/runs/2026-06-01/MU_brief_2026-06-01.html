Business Overview
  • Micron Technology is the only U.S.-based manufacturer of memory and storage semiconductors, producing DRAM, NAND, and High Bandwidth Memory (HBM) solutions for AI infrastructure, data centers, mobile, and automotive markets.
  • FY2025 was a record year: $37.4B revenue (+49% YoY), $8.5B GAAP net income - a historic recovery from the FY2023 trough ($15.5B, -49% YoY) driven almost entirely by AI server DRAM and HBM demand.
  • HBM is the pivotal product: HBM revenue grew ~50% sequentially in Q3 FY2025; entire CY2026 HBM supply is sold out under multi-customer price/volume agreements. In March 2026, Micron entered high-volume production of HBM4 for NVIDIA's Vera Rubin platform.
  • Micron holds ~21% global HBM market share (2025), up from near-zero in 2023. SK Hynix leads (~52%), Samsung trails. NVIDIA CEO Jensen Huang publicly endorsed the Micron partnership - signaling graduation from secondary to strategic supplier.

Executive Leadership
Chairman, President & CEO since 2017
Sanjay Mehrotra
Joined Micron May 2017. Previously President & CEO of SanDisk (2011-2016), which he co-founded in 1988 and sold to Western Digital for $19B. Deep NAND/storage background. Leading Micron through the AI memory supercycle with $200B domestic investment commitment.
Product & Revenue Model
  • Product sales of DRAM (76.4% of FY2025 revenue), NAND (22.7%), and other (0.9%). Revenue organized across Cloud Memory BU ($13.5B FY2025, +257% YoY), Mobile & Client BU ($11.9B, +2% YoY), and Consumer & Device BU ($7.2B, +45% YoY). Pricing driven by supply/demand dynamics.
  • HBM commands significant ASP premium over standard DRAM - key margin driver.
  • Data center share of global DRAM consumption grew from 32% (5 years ago) to ~50% (2025).

Key Risks & Debates
  • Memory cyclicality: semiconductor memory is notoriously cyclical - FY2023 ($15.5B) vs. FY2025 ($37.4B) illustrates the magnitude of swings. If AI capex decelerates or HBM oversupply emerges post-2026, Micron's revenue and margins could compress significantly
  • SK Hynix HBM leadership: SK Hynix holds ~52% global HBM market share vs. Micron's ~21% - NVIDIA's longstanding primary HBM supplier relationship with SK Hynix creates concentration risk for Micron's share gain thesis
  • China export controls: U.S.-China trade tensions and evolving export control regime create binary revenue risk for China-related sales and ongoing uncertainty for capex planning in domestic fabs
  • Capex intensity: $200B+ domestic investment commitment is massive - if the AI memory cycle turns, Micron could find itself with significant stranded capacity and high fixed costs, as it did in 2023
  • Samsung competitive response: Samsung is investing heavily in HBM ramp and process node catch-up - a successful Samsung HBM3E/HBM4 recovery could compress Micron's pricing power and market share gains
  • Customer concentration: NVIDIA and hyperscalers (Microsoft, Google, Amazon) likely represent a significant portion of HBM revenue - loss of a single large HBM customer would be disproportionately impactful

Slide-Ready Bullets
  1. Micron is the only U.S.-based volume memory manufacturer - uniquely positioned at the intersection of the AI compute buildout (HBM demand) and domestic supply chain policy (CHIPS Act, $200B capex commitment across Idaho, Virginia, and New York)
  2. FY2025 revenue of $37.4B (+49% YoY) was a record; FY2026 is tracking to ~$90B+ as Q2 FY2026 alone hit $23.9B with Q3 guided to $33.5B at ~81% gross margin - the AI memory supercycle is accelerating, not plateauing
  3. HBM4 in high-volume production for NVIDIA Vera Rubin (March 2026) - Micron was first to ship, reducing SK Hynix's near-monopoly risk and cementing a Tier-1 NVIDIA partnership. Entire CY2026 HBM supply is sold out.
  4. HBM TAM projected to grow at ~40% CAGR from ~$35B (2025) to ~$100B (2028) - Micron's ~21% share gain from near-zero in 2023 is the most significant competitive market share story in semiconductors today
  5. Key risk: memory is highly cyclical (FY2023 revenue of $15.5B, -49% YoY, illustrates the downside). The bull case depends on sustained AI capex from hyperscalers and Micron maintaining HBM market share gains vs. SK Hynix and Samsung
Smart Diligence Questions
  1. What is Micron's actual HBM revenue as a percentage of total revenue and what are the contractual terms of the CY2026 sold-out supply agreements - specifically, are these fixed-price contracts or does pricing reset, and what is the take-or-pay structure?
  2. How does Micron's HBM yield and cost structure compare to SK Hynix's at the HBM3E and HBM4 nodes? SK Hynix has a multi-year head start - if Micron's yield ramp lags, the gross margin expansion thesis could disappoint even with supply sold out.
  3. What is Micron's exposure to a potential HBM oversupply scenario in 2027-2028 as Samsung recovers and new entrants emerge - and how does the $200B domestic capex commitment constrain Micron's ability to reduce supply if the cycle turns?
