Business Overview
Plain English
  • Planet runs the largest fleet of imaging satellites on orbit.
  • Its 'Dove' satellites photograph the entire land surface of Earth nearly every day, and its higher-resolution SkySat and new Pelican satellites zoom in on specific targets.
  • It sells this imagery as a subscription, mostly to governments (defense and intelligence) and to industries like agriculture and mapping.
  • Increasingly it also sells analytics, using AI to turn the pictures into answers, and signs large multi-year deals where a government funds dedicated satellites Planet builds and operates for it.
The technical version
  • Planet operates PlanetScope (near-daily ~3m global coverage from the Dove/SuperDove fleet), SkySat (sub-meter tasking imagery/video), the next-gen Pelican high-resolution constellation (with onboard NVIDIA Jetson for edge AI), and Tanager hyperspectral satellites (VSWIR spectrometer with Carbon Mapper for methane/CO2 detection).
  • Monetization is ~98% recurring ACV across data subscriptions and a growing 'Satellite Services' model where sovereigns fund dedicated Pelican capacity and direct downlink.
  • An analytics layer ('planetary intelligence') is being built on a GPU-native AI engine with NVIDIA (Blackwell/IGX Thor) and an Anthropic (Claude) partnership; Planet is a prime on the U.S. MDA SHIELD IDIQ. FY2026 revenue $307.7M (+26%), first full year of adjusted-EBITDA profitability ($15.5M), backlog ~$900M (+79%).
Explained simply
  • Imagine a camera in space that takes a fresh photo of the whole planet every single day, and a set of sharper cameras you can point at any spot on demand.
  • Planet owns the biggest such fleet.
  • Governments and companies pay a subscription to see how the world is changing, troop movements, crops, ships, methane leaks.
  • Lately Planet does two new things: it signs big multi-year deals where a country (Germany, Japan, Sweden) pays Planet to build and fly satellites dedicated to it; and it uses AI (with Nvidia and Anthropic) to turn the flood of images into plain answers.
  • In its latest year it turned its first annual profit and its backlog of signed future business jumped almost 80%.
  • Planet Labs (NYSE: PL) operates the world's largest Earth-observation satellite fleet, imaging Earth's landmass near-daily.

Product & Revenue Model
  • Recurring data subscriptions to PlanetScope (near-daily ~3m global) and SkySat (sub-meter tasking), plus a fast-growing 'Satellite Services' model where sovereign governments fund dedicated next-gen Pelican capacity and direct downlink.

Key Risks & Debates
  • Valuation: trades at a steep EV/Rev multiple (see live comps) on a small (~$308M) revenue base; the re-rating leaves little room for execution slips.
  • Customer concentration / lumpiness: a handful of large sovereign deals (Germany, Japan, Sweden) drive growth and revenue timing; one slip or delayed recognition is material.
  • Government dependence: revenue is increasingly tied to defense budgets and procurement cycles, exposing Planet to political and budget risk.
  • Contract-duration mix: management flagged more large, shorter-term government contracts, lowering the annual/multi-year ACV share to ~85%.
  • Capex / fleet execution: Pelican and Tanager ramps require continued launches and on-orbit performance; capital-light sovereign deals mitigate but do not remove capex.
  • AI monetization is early: the NVIDIA/Anthropic 'planetary intelligence' layer is promising but not yet a proven, sized revenue line.
  • Competitive intensity: Maxar, BlackSky, Airbus, and SAR players compete for the same defense/intel budgets.
