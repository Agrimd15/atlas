Business Overview
  • Snowflake operates the AI Data Cloud - a fully managed, multi-cloud platform that unifies data warehousing, data lakes, data engineering, and AI workloads across AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud.
  • Founded in 2012, headquartered in Bozeman, MT, ~11,100 employees.
  • Core value proposition is cloud neutrality: it sits above all three hyperscalers, allowing enterprises to store, share, and query data across clouds without copying it - something AWS, Azure, and GCP structurally cannot offer without undermining their own lock-in strategies.

Product & Revenue Model
  • Snowflake runs an almost entirely consumption-based model - customers pay for compute credits and storage used, not fixed seats.
  • This creates a natural land-and-expand motion.
  • FY2026 product revenue was $4.72B (+29% YoY). Key products: Data Cloud (core warehousing), Cortex AI (LLM inference), Snowflake Intelligence (agentic AI for business users), Snowpark (data engineering + ML), Snowflake Native Apps, Streamlit. 779 customers generate >$1M in trailing product revenue.

Key Risks & Debates
  • Valuation: ~120x non-GAAP P/E keeps buy-side cautious even at 34% growth; 6 of 51 analysts still at Hold/Sell
  • New CRO risk: Jonathan Beaulier appointed March 2026 during critical AI ramp - watch GTM execution and rep retention over next 2 quarters

Slide-Ready Bullets
  1. Snowflake's consumption-based AI Data Cloud accelerated to 34% product revenue growth in Q1 FY2027 - the company's strongest sequential dollar growth in history - driven by rapid adoption of Cortex AI and Snowflake Intelligence across 9,100+ enterprise accounts
  2. A $6B multi-year AWS partnership and $200M OpenAI deal cement Snowflake's position as the neutral, model-agnostic enterprise AI data platform, structurally differentiating it from hyperscaler-native alternatives
  3. NRR of 126% and RPO of $9.2B reflect durable land-and-expand economics, with 779 customers generating >$1M in annual product revenue - a cohort expanding rapidly as AI workloads consume more compute
  4. Management raised full-year product revenue guidance to $5.84B (31% growth) and targets 13.5% non-GAAP operating margin, signaling confidence in both demand pipeline and path to profitability
  5. At 14.9x EV/FY2027E revenue, Snowflake trades at a significant discount to high-growth AI peers like Databricks (~25x) and Palantir (41x), despite re-accelerating growth and a broadening AI product portfolio
Smart Diligence Questions
  1. Databricks displacement risk: In deals where both are evaluated, what is Snowflake's current win rate and how has it trended over the last 4 quarters? What workloads are they losing most frequently?
  2. AI consumption durability: Of the 9,100+ accounts using AI products, what percentage have moved beyond pilot to production workloads generating meaningful consumption revenue - and what does the ramp curve look like from activation to $100K+ spend?
  3. CRO transition impact: What is the pipeline coverage ratio and average sales cycle for $1M+ deals heading into Q2, given the CRO change? Has there been any rep attrition in enterprise accounts?
