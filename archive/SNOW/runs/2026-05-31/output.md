## 1. Business Overview
Snowflake operates the AI Data Cloud — a fully managed, multi-cloud platform that unifies data warehousing, data lakes, data engineering, and AI workloads across AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud. Founded in 2012, headquartered in Bozeman, MT, ~11,100 employees. The company's core value proposition is cloud neutrality: it sits above all three hyperscalers, allowing enterprises to store, share, and query data across clouds without copying it — something AWS, Azure, and GCP structurally cannot offer without undermining their own lock-in strategies.

## 2. Product & Revenue Model
Snowflake runs an almost entirely consumption-based model — customers pay for compute credits and storage used, not fixed seats. This creates a natural land-and-expand motion: customers start small and grow as data workloads increase. FY2026 product revenue was $4.72B (+29% YoY). A small professional services segment (~$212M) rounds out the total. 779 customers now generate >$1M in trailing product revenue, up significantly YoY.

**Key products:** Data Cloud (core warehousing), Cortex AI (LLM inference + AI features), Snowflake Intelligence (agentic AI for business users), Snowpark (data engineering + ML), Snowflake Native Apps, Streamlit.

## 5. Key Risks / Debates
- **Valuation:** ~120x non-GAAP P/E keeps a segment of buy-side cautious even at 34% growth. 6 of 51 analysts still at Hold/Sell.
- **New CRO risk:** Leadership transition during AI ramp — execution risk worth monitoring over next 2 quarters.
- **Databricks competition:** Databricks growing at 65% (vs. SNOW's 34%) and pushing into Snowflake's core SQL/warehouse territory. Private, so harder to track.
- **Consumption model volatility:** Unlike SaaS seats, consumption revenue can decelerate rapidly if enterprises optimize workloads or reduce cloud spend.
- **Margin expansion pace:** Some investors focused on whether margin expansion is keeping pace with the raised revenue outlook.

- Snowflake's consumption-based AI Data Cloud accelerated to 34% product revenue growth in Q1 FY2027 — the company's strongest sequential dollar growth in history — driven by rapid adoption of Cortex AI and Snowflake Intelligence across 9,100+ enterprise accounts
- A $6B multi-year AWS partnership and $200M OpenAI deal cement Snowflake's position as the neutral, model-agnostic enterprise AI data platform, structurally differentiating it from hyperscaler-native alternatives
- NRR of 126% and RPO of $9.2B reflect durable land-and-expand economics, with 779 customers generating >$1M in annual product revenue — a cohort expanding rapidly as AI workloads consume more compute
- Management raised full-year product revenue guidance to $5.84B (31% growth) and targets 13.5% non-GAAP operating margin, signaling confidence in both demand pipeline and path to profitability
- At 14.9x EV/FY2027E revenue, Snowflake trades at a significant discount to high-growth AI peers like Databricks (~25x, private) and Palantir (41x), despite re-accelerating growth and a broadening AI product portfolio

## 8. Three Smart Diligence Questions
1. **Databricks displacement risk:** In deals where both Databricks and Snowflake are evaluated, what is Snowflake's current win rate and how has it trended over the last 4 quarters? What workloads are they losing most frequently?
2. **AI consumption durability:** Of the 9,100+ accounts using AI products, what percentage have moved beyond pilot/trial to production workloads generating meaningful consumption revenue — and what does the ramp curve look like from activation to $100K+ spend?
3. **CRO transition impact:** What is the pipeline coverage ratio and average sales cycle for Snowflake's $1M+ deals heading into Q2, given the CRO change? Has there been any rep attrition in enterprise accounts?
